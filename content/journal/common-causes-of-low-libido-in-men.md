Low libido, or decreased interest in sexual activity, is something that may affect anyone from time to time as libido varies with age and even circumstances, though if you are finding that your interest in sex has decreased and stayed low for a lengthened period of time, it may be cause for concern. Here are some common causes of low libido in men.

### Testosterone 

Testosterone is an important hormone for men as it is thought to regulate libido, bone mass, fat distribution, muscle mass and strength, and the production of red blood cells and sperm. A Decrease in testosterone is a normal part of aging but large drops in testosterone can lead to decrease libido. 

### Medications 

Taking certain medications can lower testosterone levels, which in turn may lead to low libido and the inability to ejaculate or become erect. Such medications include ACE inhibitors and beta-blockers, chemotherapy or radiation treatment for cancer, hormones used to treat prostate cancer, corticosteroids, opioid pain relievers, and certain antidepressants.

### Depression and Stress 

Life changes that impact mood is highly likely to impact your interest in sex. When undergoing periods of high stress, hormone levels are also disrupted which may affect libido. People who suffer from depression have a decreased or total lack of interest in activities they previously found pleasurable, such as sex. 

Low libido is also a common side effect of some antidepressants, specifically SNRIs and SSRIs. 

Alcohol and Drug use The over consumption of alcohol and the use of tobacco, marijuana, or illicit drugs may all lessen the libido and even cause dampened sperm production and movement. 

Other causes include low self-esteem, not getting enough sleep, a lack of or too much exercise, and aging. No matter the cause, so the effects of low libido often leave a man in a cycle of negative feelings which only worsen the symptoms.
