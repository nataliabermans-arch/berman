## What Is [Bioidentical Hormone Replacement Therapy](/biote-hormone-therapy/) (BHRT)?

If you’re dealing with the frustrating symptoms of menopause, you’ve likely heard about hormone replacement therapy. Bioidentical Hormone Replacement Therapy, or BHRT, is a specific approach to this treatment. Think of it as a way to replenish the hormones your body naturally produces but is now making less of. As your ovaries slow down production of hormones like estrogen and progesterone, you can experience everything from hot flashes and night sweats to mood swings and brain fog.

BHRT uses hormones that are molecularly identical to the ones your body makes. Because their structure is the same, your body can recognize and use them just as it would its own. The goal of [Bioidentical Hormone Replacement Therapy](/biote-hormone-therapy/) is to restore your hormonal balance, which can help you find significant relief from uncomfortable symptoms. It’s not about changing who you are; it’s about helping you feel like yourself again so you can get back to living your life with energy and confidence.

### BHRT vs. Synthetic Hormones: What’s the Difference?

The terms “bioidentical” and “synthetic” can be confusing, so let’s clear things up. “Bioidentical” simply means the hormone has the exact same molecular structure as the hormones your body produces. This allows your body to metabolize them in a familiar way.

Many people assume bioidentical hormones are “natural” and synthetic ones are not, but it’s a bit more nuanced. Bioidentical hormones are often derived from plant sources and then synthesized in a lab to match human hormones perfectly. The key isn’t the source, but the final structure. Some conventional, FDA-approved hormone therapies are also bioidentical. The main distinction is that BHRT often involves custom-compounded hormones tailored specifically to your individual needs, based on your lab results.

## Finding Your Fit: BHRT Delivery Methods

Once you’ve decided to move forward with bioidentical hormone replacement therapy, the next step is figuring out how you’ll take it. BHRT isn’t a one-size-fits-all solution, and the way hormones are delivered into your body can be customized to fit your lifestyle and specific needs. The goal is to find a method that feels comfortable, convenient, and effective for you.

From pellets that offer a steady, long-term dose to daily creams that become part of your routine, each option has its own advantages. Working with your doctor, you can explore these choices to find the perfect match for your health goals. Let’s walk through some of the most common delivery methods so you can feel prepared for that conversation.

### Hormone Pellets

If you prefer a low-maintenance approach, hormone pellets might be the right fit. These are tiny, custom-compounded pellets, often smaller than a grain of rice, that are placed just under the skin during a simple in-office procedure. They work by slowly and consistently releasing hormones directly into your bloodstream over several months. This method helps you avoid the daily fluctuations that can come with other forms and means you don’t have to remember a daily pill or cream. Dr. Berman offers different hormone delivery systems, including [Biote Hormone Therapy](/hormone-therapy/) pellets, to ensure stable hormone levels.

### Creams, Gels, and Patches

For those who prefer a non-invasive, daily application, creams, gels, and patches are excellent options. These are applied directly to the skin, allowing the hormones to be absorbed into the bloodstream. This method, known as transdermal delivery, is great because it’s easy to incorporate into your morning or evening routine. It also gives your doctor the flexibility to adjust your dosage as needed. Whether it’s a patch you change a couple of times a week or a cream you apply daily, these methods offer a straightforward way to manage your [bioidentical hormone replacement therapy](/biote-hormone-therapy/).

### Oral Medications and Other Options

Oral medications, like pills or capsules, are another common choice for BHRT. This traditional method is simple and familiar for many people. Beyond pills, there are also more targeted options available, such as vaginal inserts or creams. These are particularly helpful for addressing localized symptoms like vaginal dryness or discomfort without affecting the entire body. These varied options ensure that your treatment can be tailored precisely to your symptoms, including providing effective [vaginal dryness treatment](/vaginal-dryness-treatment/) where it’s needed most.

With several options available, how do you decide which one is best? The answer comes from a thoughtful conversation with your healthcare provider. The right method for you depends on your medical history, your specific symptoms, your hormone levels, and, just as importantly, your personal preferences and lifestyle.
