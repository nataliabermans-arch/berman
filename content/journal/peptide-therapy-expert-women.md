### What Are Peptides and What Do They Do?

So, what exactly are peptides? In simple terms, they are tiny messengers made of short chains of amino acids, which are the building blocks of proteins. Your body produces peptides naturally, and they play a crucial role in directing your cells and molecules to perform specific jobs. They are essential for countless bodily functions, from managing your metabolism and immune response to repairing skin, joints, and muscles after an injury.

Because they are so fundamental to our biology, using them in therapy is a way of speaking your body’s own language. Peptide treatments can help promote healing, increase collagen production for firmer skin, and support lean muscle mass. They are a precise tool for optimizing your health from the inside out.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How is [peptide therapy](/biote-hormone-therapy/) different from hormone replacement therapy?** Think of it this way: hormone replacement therapy helps restore the foundational hormone levels your body needs to function well, like estrogen or testosterone. Peptide therapy is more like a specialized messaging service. It uses specific peptides to give your cells very precise instructions, such as “build more collagen” or “regulate appetite.” The two therapies work beautifully together, with hormones setting the stage and peptides fine-tuning the performance.

**Do I have to do injections, or are there other options?** While small, simple injections are the most common and effective way to administer peptides, they aren’t the only choice. Injections ensure the peptides are absorbed directly and can get to work right away. However, depending on the specific peptide and your personal goals, other methods like topical creams or nasal sprays may be available. This is something you can discuss with your provider to find a method that feels comfortable and fits into your routine.

**How long will it take to see results, and are they permanent?** The timeline really depends on your body and your goals. Some women notice improvements in their sleep and energy levels within a few weeks. Other benefits, like changes in skin texture or body composition, can take a couple of months of consistent use to become apparent. The results are not permanent; they last as long as you continue the therapy. If you stop, your body will slowly return to its previous state, which is why many women see it as an ongoing part of their wellness plan.

**Is peptide therapy only for women going through menopause?** Not at all. While it is an excellent tool for managing menopause symptoms like hot flashes and brain fog, its benefits are much broader. Women at various stages of life use peptide therapy to achieve specific goals. This can include anything from improving skin elasticity and supporting weight management to enhancing libido and speeding up recovery from workouts. The treatment is customized to you, not your age.

**What happens if I decide to stop the therapy?** You can stop peptide therapy at any time without experiencing withdrawal symptoms. It is not a medication that creates dependency. Over time, the benefits you’ve gained from the therapy will gradually fade as your body’s cellular functions return to their baseline. The process is gentle, and you can always decide to restart the therapy later if you miss the way you felt while on your personalized plan.
