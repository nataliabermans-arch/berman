To find your ideal dosage, your doctor will almost always begin with a “start low and go slow” approach. For weekly injections, a typical starting dose is usually between 2 to 4 milligrams. It allows your doctor to see exactly how your body responds and gives you both time to assess any changes in your symptoms and overall well-being. From there, your dosage can be fine-tuned based on follow-up blood work and how you’re feeling. ### Cypionate vs. Enanthate: What’s the Difference?

When you look at your prescription, you’ll likely see the name testosterone cypionate or testosterone enanthate. These are the two most common forms of injectable testosterone used in hormone therapy. While they have different names, they are incredibly similar in their chemical structure and how they work in the body. Both are effective, high-quality options that are often used interchangeably. Both cypionate and enanthate are forms of bioidentical testosterone attached to an ester, which is a compound that helps control the hormone’s release into your bloodstream. This ensures a slow, steady delivery, which helps maintain stable hormone levels between injections. Your doctor will choose the one that’s best suited for your specific treatment plan, but you can feel confident that both are excellent choices for therapy.

### Creams, Gels, and Pills

Topical testosterone, in the form of a cream or gel, is a popular alternative to injections. This method involves applying a small, precise dose to your skin daily, usually on the inner thigh or upper arm. Many women find that creams and gels are particularly effective for bringing back libido, with a common daily dose starting around 5 milligrams. It’s important to know that your skin only absorbs about 15-20% of the testosterone you apply, which is why consistent, daily use is key to maintaining stable hormone levels. While oral testosterone pills exist, they are prescribed less frequently for women because they have to be processed by the liver, which can add unnecessary strain. For many, the ease and gentle absorption of a topical cream is an ideal way to get effective [treatment for low libido in females](/treatment-for-low-libido-in-females/).

#### Understanding Absorption and DHT Conversion

When you use a topical testosterone, your body’s unique chemistry comes into play. The rate at which your skin absorbs the hormone can vary, and a portion of the testosterone can be converted into a more potent androgen called dihydrotestosterone (DHT). While this is a natural process, it’s something your doctor will monitor to ensure your hormonal balance remains optimal. This is also a good time to remember that while testosterone therapy can be a powerful tool for improving sexual satisfaction, it’s one piece of a larger wellness picture. ### A Word of Caution on Testosterone Pellets

Testosterone pellets, which are inserted under the skin and release hormones over several months, are another option you may have heard about. They offer the convenience of not needing to think about daily or weekly dosing. However, it’s important to approach this method with a full understanding of the potential downsides. Some experts advise caution with testosterone pellets because they are not regulated with the same oversight as other forms of therapy. Once a pellet is inserted, it cannot be removed, and the dose can’t be adjusted until it’s time for a new one. This lack of flexibility can be a problem if you experience side effects, as the hormone release can sometimes be unpredictable.
