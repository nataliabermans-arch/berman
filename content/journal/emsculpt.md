> ## Why Women Struggle to Lose Weight

No one is immune to weight gain, and as we age, it’s harder to maintain that healthy weight. Even though both men and women face this struggle, recent research suggests that women may lose weight differently than men. While our genetics, daily caloric intake, the foods we eat and our activity level all play a role in metabolism, women face additional factors that prevent them from losing weight. Some of these include:

- Muscle Mass – Generally speaking, women tend to be smaller in size than men and have less muscle and more fat. As a result, men typically lose weight faster while eating more calories daily than women. Muscle burns more calories than fat, so men are usually able to burn more calories at rest than women. Emscupt builds lean muscle mass while toning your body and increases the number of calories you burn at rest.
- Menstrual Cycles – While your menstrual cycle itself doesn’t contribute to weight loss or gain, it can affect your weight in other ways. You may already be all too aware that premenstrual syndrome (PMS) can cause you to crave and eat more sugar or salty foods than you usually do, which can lead to weight gain. Salty foods also cause the body to retain more water, which can raise the number you see on the scale.
- Menopause – It can definitely be challenging to maintain and/or lose weight during menopause; most women will gain an average of five to ten pounds and fat tends to build up in the abdominal region. As we age, our metabolism begins to slow down due to a decline in ovarian hormones.

As you can see from the list, some of these factors you have some control over — others you do not. Perhaps it is not entirely your fault that you’ve put on some additional weight, and perhaps there are some areas you could improve. At the same time, even if you are able to control the weight you lose through a healthy diet and active lifestyle, you can’t always control where that weight comes off. Common trouble spots such as the midsection or backside don’t always receive the full benefits of your efforts. The good news is EMSCULPT picks up where your workouts left off to help you shape and tone the areas that trouble you the most.

## What is EMSculpt?

EMSCULPT is a noninvasive body contouring treatment created by BTL, a world-renowned manufacturer of medical devices. Unlike other noninvasive body contouring treatments that focus exclusively on reducing unwanted fat, EMSCULPT builds muscle in those hard-to-tone areas to take your results to the next level. The proprietary technology utilized by EMSCULPT eliminates the need for uncomfortable heat-inducing treatments such as lasers, radiofrequency or ultrasound energy to break up fat cells. Instead, EMSCULPT works with the natural processes of your body to simulate an intense workout, just at a much faster and more intense rate than you could ever achieve manually at the gym.

The “EM” in EMSCULPT stands for “electromagnetic”; the secret behind the device’s success. More specifically, EMSCULPT uses revolutionary high-intensity focused electromagnetic (HIFEM) energy similar to that used in an MRI machine to produce supramaximal muscle contractions in the targeted abdominal or gluteal muscles during treatment. Superamaxial contractions refer to high-tension muscle contractions that exceed the highest amount of tension your muscles can exert and hold under normal conditions, or your maximal voluntary contractions (MVC). EMSCULPT triggers up to 20,000 supramaximal contractions in a single treatment session or the equivalent of 20,000 crunches or squats in just 30 minutes.

When exposed to these contractions, your muscle tissue is forced to adapt to these extreme conditions. This, in turn, causes the targeted muscles to start remodeling their internal structure. In other words, EMSCULPT increases your muscle volume and density. EMSCULPT is the only FDA-cleared device today designed to build muscle mass and, in turn, eliminate stubborn fat without downtime or discomfort.

> ## Is EMSCULPT Right For You?

Because EMSCULPT treatments are nonsurgical, it does not require any anesthesia or incisions. While EMSCULPT is safe and noninvasive, some contraindications would prevent you from undergoing treatment. If you have any electronic or metal implants inside your body, such as a pacemaker, metal IUD or other medical device, the device will cause metal to heat and electronics to short-circuit. Women who are pregnant will also not be able to undergo EMSCULPT. My staff and I will take the time to go over your medical history and goals prior to your treatment to determine whether EMSCULPT is the right choice for you at this time.

> ## What to Expect From the EMSCULPT Procedure

EMSCULPT treatments are quick and relatively comfortable. Most patients liken the sensations experienced during their procedure to that of an intense workout at the gym. During an EMSCULPT session, all that will be required of you is to lay down and take it easy as the device does all of the work for you. The specially designed paddles of EMSCULPT will be placed over your abdomen or gluteal muscles depending on the area you have selected to target.

We will start the treatment on a lower setting to allow you to get used to the sensation, then gradually increase the intensity according to your tolerance and desired results. The device alternates between an active phase and a resting phase to ensure your muscles get all the benefits of a simulated workout as lie there. Although the feeling of involuntary muscle contractions can feel odd at first, you will quickly adjust to the sensations as your treatment progresses. One EMSCULPT session is typically just 30 minutes in duration, making it the perfect lunch-break treatment or quick enough to fit into your already busy schedule.

> ## What Are the Potential Side Effects of EMSCULPT?

As a gentle muscle toning treatment, EMSCULPT produces virtually zero side effects afterward. You may feel a bit sore in your treated area following your treatment sessions. Because the targeted muscles are exposed to thousands of contractions in such a short period, patients can expect to experience some muscle fatigue similar to how you feel after a vigorous workout at the gym. Any discomfort or soreness should not persist for more than a few days, and will not keep you from performing your regular day-to-day activities.
