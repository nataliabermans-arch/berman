### **Menopause Symptoms May Come Back After Stopping Menopausal Hormone Therapy**

Some women who use a combination of estrogen and progestin to control the symptoms of [menopause](/menopause/) might find symptoms return when they stop the hormones. According to the latest findings from the Women’s Health Initiative (WHI), a major clinical trial of the risks and benefits of menopausal hormone therapy supported by the National Institutes of Health (NIH).

### Frequently Asked Questions About Hormone Therapy

**Q: What is the difference between Bioidentical and Synthetic hormones?** Bioidentical hormones are derived from plant sources and are engineered to be chemically identical to the hormones produced by the human body. Synthetic hormones (like Premarin) often use animal-derived estrogens that do not perfectly match human receptors, often leading to more side effects.

**Q: Is Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) safe?** Recent studies, including re-evaluations of the Women’s Health Initiative (WHI), show that BHRT is safe for most women when started early and monitored by a specialist. Dr. Berman uses a “whole-woman” approach to minimize risks.

**Q: How long does it take to see results from BHRT?** While some women feel relief from night sweats within days, it typically takes 3 to 6 weeks to feel the full benefits of energy, mood, and libido improvements.
