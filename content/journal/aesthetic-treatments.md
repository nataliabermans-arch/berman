## What Is the Clamshell Exercise?

The clamshell exercise is a simple yet highly effective movement designed to strengthen the muscles around your hips, glutes, and pelvis. You perform it by lying on your side with your knees bent and stacked, then lifting your top knee towards the ceiling while keeping your feet together—just like a clamshell opening. This exercise specifically targets the gluteus medius, a crucial muscle for maintaining hip stability and proper pelvic alignment. Its low-impact nature makes it an excellent choice for nearly everyone, regardless of fitness level. You don’t need any special equipment, so you can easily add it to your daily routine to build foundational strength for better overall movement and function, as explained by many [physical therapy experts](https://www.healthline.com/health/fitness-exercise/the-clamshell-exercise).

Beyond general fitness, strengthening your hips with clamshells offers significant benefits for women’s wellness. Strong hip muscles are essential for a stable pelvis, which in turn supports the pelvic floor. This internal support system is vital for managing and preventing issues like urinary incontinence and can also help alleviate certain types of pelvic discomfort. By creating better muscle balance and stability, this simple exercise empowers you to take a proactive role in your health. It serves as a great complementary practice alongside professional treatments, helping you build a resilient foundation for your body and move through life with greater confidence and control. ### How to Perform the Clamshell Exercise Correctly

While professional treatments can work wonders, complementing them with targeted exercises at home can help you maintain your results and support your overall wellness. The clamshell is a fantastic, low-impact move for strengthening your hips, glutes, and pelvic floor. To do it correctly, lie on your side with your legs stacked and knees bent at a 45-degree angle. Rest your head on your bottom arm and use your top arm for stability. It’s crucial to keep your hip bones stacked one on top of the other. Before you start, engage your core by pulling your belly button toward your spine. Keeping your feet together, slowly lift your top knee as high as you can without your hips rolling back. Pause at the top, then lower your knee back down. Strong hip muscles are essential for stability and can even help with issues like [pelvic pain](/pelvic-pain-treatment/).

### Variations and Progressions to Increase the Challenge

Once you feel comfortable with the basic clamshell and can perform it with perfect form, you might be ready to take it up a notch. Adding variations can prevent plateaus and continue to challenge your muscles, leading to greater strength and stability. These progressions build on the foundational movement, targeting your glutes and core in new ways. Trying different versions keeps your routine interesting and ensures you keep making progress toward your fitness goals. Here are a few simple ways to increase the intensity of your clamshell exercise.

#### With a Resistance Band

To really fire up your glutes, add a resistance band. Loop a band around both thighs, positioning it just above your knees. The added resistance will force your muscles to work harder as you lift your top knee, making the exercise significantly more effective for building strength in your glutes and hamstrings. This is one of the most popular ways to [progress the clamshell](https://www.hingehealth.com/resources/articles/clamshell/) and you’ll definitely feel the difference.

#### With Feet Lifted

For another simple but effective progression, try lifting your feet off the floor. Maintain the same side-lying position, but elevate both feet so they are hovering in the air throughout the entire movement. As you open and close your knees, keeping your feet lifted forces your stabilizing muscles, especially in your bottom glute, to work harder to keep you balanced. It’s a small adjustment that delivers a much bigger challenge.

#### Side Plank Clamshell

If you want to engage your core more directly, the side plank clamshell is an excellent choice. Start in a modified side plank, supporting your upper body on your forearm with your knees bent and stacked. From this position, lift your hips off the floor and then perform the clamshell motion by raising your top knee. This variation not only strengthens your hips and glutes but also builds serious oblique and core stability.

#### Weighted Clamshell

When you’re ready for a serious challenge, introduce a weight. You can hold a small dumbbell (start with 3-8 pounds) on the side of your top thigh as you perform the exercise. This added load requires much more strength and control to lift your knee, making it an advanced variation for building powerful glutes. Just be sure to maintain proper form and keep your hips stable throughout the movement.

## Important Considerations for the Clamshell Exercise

While the clamshell is a fantastic exercise for building hip and glute strength, it’s not a one-size-fits-all movement. Like any exercise, proper form and awareness are key to getting the benefits without causing harm. Before you add it to your routine, it’s smart to understand the potential risks and how it compares to other common lower-body exercises. This ensures you’re making the best choice for your body and your fitness goals, helping you stay safe and strong on your wellness journey.

### Potential Risks and When to Be Cautious

It’s important to listen to your body, as the clamshell exercise can sometimes make existing injuries worse. If you have conditions like gluteal tendinopathy or hip bursitis, this movement might irritate the already sensitive tendons or bursae in your hip. Pay close attention to how you feel. If you notice your hips rocking backward as you lift your knee, you could be putting unnecessary strain on your back. A pinch in your groin is another signal to adjust your form, as everyone’s hip anatomy is slightly different. To stay safe, always [activate](https://www.sports-injury-physio.com/post/the-clamshells-exercise-benefits-variation-and-progressions) your core by pulling your belly button toward your spine. And if lying on a hard floor is uncomfortable, don’t hesitate to use a thick mat or even do the exercise on your bed.

### Clamshells vs. Squats: A Low-Impact Alternative

If high-impact exercises like squats or lunges aren’t for you, the clamshell is an excellent alternative. It’s a simple, low-impact movement that effectively targets your outer thighs and hip muscles without putting stress on your joints. This makes it a great option for anyone dealing with joint pain or recovering from an injury. While squats work larger muscle groups, clamshells isolate the hip abductors, which are crucial for stability and posture. Think of them as a simple but powerful tool for improving your hip health and overall lower body strength. By incorporating clamshells, you can [improve](https://www.americansportandfitness.com/blogs/fitness-blog/the-top-10-benefits-of-clamshells?srsltid=AfmBOoqrFPRxlAmFY_j_Rpzn0vowX1FHnxPyfy1SDz5-ajwYLsgii1pb) your body’s foundation in a gentle yet effective way.
