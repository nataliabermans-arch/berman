## When Do Our Hormones Start to Change?

Fertility technically begins to decline in our late twenties. Hormone imbalance, fluctuations, and ultimately declining levels occur with age. It’s a gradual process, so many don’t feel the effects of this decline until they hit their late thirties or early forties. 

### The Scope of Menopause in the U.S.

If you’re feeling the effects of hormonal changes, you’re far from alone. In the United States, about 1 million women [enter menopause](https://www.interiorwomenshealth.org/blog/5-reasons-biote-hormone-replacement-therapy-may-be-right-for-you) each year, making it a shared and significant life transition. As we approach this stage, our bodies naturally produce less estrogen and progesterone, which can throw our systems out of whack. This hormonal shift is behind many of the frustrating symptoms that can disrupt daily life, from mood swings and anxiety to sleep disturbances and a decreased libido. Thankfully, you don’t have to simply endure the discomfort. Treatments like [Biote Hormone Therapy](/hormone-therapy/) use bioidentical hormones to gently restore your natural balance, helping to alleviate the symptoms tied to menopause and get you back to feeling like yourself.

## What Are the Signs of a Hormone Imbalance?

You probably have hormonal imbalance if you are experiencing at least two or three of the following symptoms:

1.  Decline in short-term memory, focus, or concentration
2.  Decline in your physical energy level
3.  Decline in sexual function, response, and/or sexual desire
4.  Weight gain
5.  Loose crepey skin, hair loss
6.  Mood swings

Some additional symptoms include night sweats, hot flashes, and urinary or vaginal symptoms. It’s important to watch out for these symptoms and seek treatment as soon as possible. That’s where BioTE comes in!

### Symptoms in Women

For women, the signs of hormonal imbalance often become most apparent during perimenopause and menopause. You might experience classic symptoms like hot flashes and night sweats, but the effects can be much broader, touching everything from your mood to your sexual health. Many women also report feelings of anxiety, persistent fatigue, and a frustrating inability to focus. BioTE is a type of hormone replacement therapy that helps women deal with these symptoms. It uses “bioidentical” hormones, which means they are chemically exactly like the hormones your body naturally produces. By restoring optimal levels of estrogen and testosterone, this therapy can help you manage these changes and feel more like yourself again, addressing concerns like [vaginal dryness](/vaginal-dryness-treatment/) and a decreased interest in sex.

### Symptoms in Men

It’s a common misconception that significant hormonal shifts are exclusive to women. Men also experience a natural decline in hormone levels, a process often called andropause. BioTE Hormone Therapy helps fix hormone imbalances that can happen as people get older, using “bioidentical hormones” that are very similar to the ones the body makes naturally. For men, this decline in testosterone can lead to symptoms like a noticeable drop in energy, loss of muscle mass, increased body fat (especially around the midsection), and moodiness or irritability. These changes can be gradual, making them easy to dismiss as just “part of getting older,” but they are often directly linked to a hormonal imbalance that can be addressed.

#### Addressing Andropause

When it comes to andropause, some of the most impactful symptoms are related to sexual health. As testosterone levels fall, many men experience a lower sex drive and may find it more difficult to maintain erections. This can be a sensitive topic, but it’s a very real and common physical issue. BioTE aims to bring these levels back into balance, which can help with issues like [low libido](/low-libido-and-your-relationships/) and erectile dysfunction. By restoring testosterone to its optimal range, men can often regain their vitality and confidence, which benefits not only them but their relationships as well. Understanding these changes is key to addressing them effectively.

### Other Physical Symptoms

Beyond the gender-specific signs, hormone imbalances can cause many uncomfortable symptoms that affect your overall quality of life. Issues like unexplained weight gain, persistent fatigue that sleep doesn’t seem to fix, and trouble concentrating can make daily tasks feel monumental. You might also experience mood swings that feel completely out of your control. These symptoms are often interconnected; for example, poor sleep caused by night sweats can lead to fatigue and brain fog the next day. Addressing the root cause—the hormonal imbalance—with a personalized treatment like [Biote Hormone Therapy](/hormone-therapy/) can help alleviate this entire cluster of symptoms, allowing you to feel more stable and energized.

#### Joint Pain

One of the more surprising symptoms of hormonal imbalance is joint pain. If you’ve started to feel achy and stiff for no apparent reason, fluctuating hormone levels could be the culprit. Many common problems people face as they age, from feeling tired to gaining weight, might be caused by hormones that are out of balance, and joint pain is one of them. Estrogen, in particular, plays a role in managing inflammation throughout the body. When estrogen levels decline during menopause, inflammation can increase, leading to discomfort in your joints. Restoring hormonal balance can help reduce this inflammation, offering relief from aches and pains you might have thought were just an inevitable part of aging.

### Potential Side Effects of Hormone Therapy

When starting hormone therapy, your body may go through an adjustment period as it gets used to the new hormone levels. During this time, some people might experience temporary side effects. These can include things like breast tenderness, mild fluid retention, acne, or mood swings. For most, these effects are mild and tend to resolve as your body adapts to the therapy. Open communication with your provider is key, as dosages can be fine-tuned to minimize any discomfort. A carefully managed plan ensures that any side effects are monitored and addressed promptly, making the transition as smooth as possible.
