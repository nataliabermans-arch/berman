### Can You Get Emsella While Pregnant or Breastfeeding?

If you are pregnant, might be pregnant, or are currently breastfeeding, you’ll need to wait to schedule Emsella treatments. The effects of the powerful electromagnetic energy on a developing fetus have not been studied, so for the safety of you and your baby, it’s a firm contraindication. Similarly, it’s best to wait until you have finished nursing before beginning treatment. This time is all about focusing on your health and your little one. ### Why Electronic Implants Rule Out Emsella

Because Emsella uses powerful electromagnetic currents, it can interfere with the function of any electronic implants in your body. This is a major safety concern, so the treatment is not an option for anyone with devices like pacemakers, implantable defibrillators, or neurostimulators. The HIFEM technology that makes [Emsella Incontinence Therapy](/emsella-treatment-for-incontinence/) so effective at stimulating your muscles could potentially disrupt or damage these essential medical devices. It’s vital to disclose any and all implanted electronics during your consultation to ensure your safety is never compromised.

### What About Metal Implants or Plates?

Just as electronic implants are a contraindication, so are certain metal implants. The electromagnetic energy used in Emsella can cause the metal to heat up, which could lead to discomfort or internal burns. This includes things like metal plates or screws from a past surgery, joint replacements (especially in the hip area), or a copper IUD. If you have any metal hardware in your body, particularly in the pelvic or abdominal region, Emsella is not a safe option for you. Always be sure to discuss any past surgeries or implants with your doctor to determine if this treatment is appropriate.
