### First, What Is Pelvic Floor Dysfunction?

Your pelvic floor is a group of muscles that acts like a supportive hammock for your pelvic organs, including your bladder, uterus, and bowels. When these muscles weaken or become damaged—often due to factors like childbirth, menopause, or aging—it can lead to pelvic floor dysfunction. This can show up as urinary leakage, a frequent need to urinate, or even pelvic pain. It’s an incredibly common issue, but that doesn’t mean you have to live with it. Addressing [incontinence](/emsella-treatment-for-incontinence/) and other symptoms starts with strengthening these essential muscles, which is exactly what Emsella is designed to do.

#### How Common Are Pelvic Floor Issues?

If you’re dealing with pelvic floor issues, it’s easy to feel like you’re the only one. But the reality is, it’s incredibly common. Research shows that [over one-third of women in the U.S.](https://www.bcm.edu/research/research-centers/center-for-research-on-women-with-disabilities/a-to-z-directory/pelvic-health/pelvic-floor-disorders/prevalence) experience some form of pelvic floor disorder. The numbers are even higher for new mothers, with nearly half facing these challenges within a decade of giving birth. These conditions, especially urinary [incontinence](/emsella-treatment-for-incontinence/), are a significant health concern for women of all ages, not just something to be accepted as a normal part of aging or motherhood. Understanding just how widespread these issues are is the first step toward seeking a solution and realizing you don’t have to simply put up with the discomfort or inconvenience.

### Can Emsella Help with Incontinence?

Emsella is effective for the most common types of urinary incontinence women experience. This includes **stress incontinence**, which is when you leak a little urine during activities that put pressure on your bladder, like coughing, sneezing, laughing, or exercising. It also treats **urge incontinence**, characterized by a sudden, intense urge to urinate followed by an involuntary loss of urine. Many women experience a combination of both, known as **mixed incontinence**. By strengthening the entire pelvic floor, Emsella helps restore neuromuscular control, addressing the core issue behind these frustrating and often unpredictable leaks.
