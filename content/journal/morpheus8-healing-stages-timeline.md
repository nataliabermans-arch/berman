## Your Morpheus8 Aftercare Guide

Getting the most out of your Morpheus8 treatment doesn’t stop when you leave the office. How you care for your skin in the days and weeks that follow is crucial for a smooth healing process and achieving those incredible, long-lasting results. Think of it as protecting your investment. ### Modifying Your Skincare Routine

This is the time to simplify. Your skin is in a delicate healing phase, so your regular, high-performance products need to take a temporary backseat. For the first week, your routine should focus entirely on gentle cleansing, hydration, and protection. We’ll help you build a temporary regimen using soothing products that support the skin’s recovery without causing irritation. Once you’re fully healed, we can discuss an ongoing skincare plan tailored to your needs. The right [expert care](/about/) and products will help you maintain and even enhance your Morpheus8 results for months and years to come, keeping your skin firm, smooth, and radiant.

### Protecting Your Skin: Sunscreen and Makeup Rules

Think of sunscreen as your skin’s new best friend. After Morpheus8, your skin is especially vulnerable to sun damage. You’ll need to apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher every single day, even if you’re just staying indoors. Reapply it often and try to avoid direct sun exposure and tanning while you heal. As for makeup, it’s best to go bare-faced for at least 24 to 48 hours to let your skin breathe. When you are ready to wear makeup again, opt for a mineral-based formula. It’s generally lighter and less likely to irritate your healing skin. Always check with your provider before applying any cosmetics.

### Activities and Products to Pause During Healing

To give your skin the best environment for healing, you’ll need to press pause on a few things. For at least a week, avoid any harsh or active skincare products. This includes exfoliants, scrubs, retinol or retinoids, and any acids like glycolic or salicylic acid. These can be too aggressive for your sensitive skin and may interfere with the healing process. It’s also a good idea to skip strenuous workouts, saunas, steam rooms, and swimming for the first few days. Anything that causes excessive sweating or heat can increase swelling and irritation. Gentle walks are fine, but save the high-intensity session for next week.

### The Role of Hydration and Gentle Cleansing

Hydration is key to a speedy recovery. You can support your skin from the inside out by drinking plenty of water throughout the day—this helps your body’s natural healing mechanisms function at their best. Topically, apply a gentle, soothing moisturizer frequently to keep the skin hydrated and comfortable, which can also help with any feelings of tightness. When it’s time to wash your face, use a mild, non-abrasive cleanser and lukewarm water. Gently pat your skin dry with a clean towel; never rub. This simple, supportive approach ensures your skin gets exactly what it needs to rebuild and reveal its renewed, revitalized appearance.
