## What Is Aviva Non-Surgical Labiaplasty?

If you’re exploring options for labiaplasty, you’ve likely come across both traditional surgery and newer, less invasive methods. ### How Does Radio-Frequency Technology Work?

So, how does heat actually reshape tissue? The technology behind Aviva is grounded in the science of radio-frequency assisted lipolysis (RFAL). A very thin probe is inserted under the skin, where it delivers controlled RF energy directly to the targeted tissue. This energy creates a gentle heating effect that causes two things to happen. First, it immediately makes the existing collagen fibers contract and the tissue shrink. Second, it triggers your body’s natural healing response, stimulating the production of new, firming collagen over the following weeks and months. This dual action allows for both immediate and long-term improvements in the shape and tightness of the labia.

### Aviva vs. Surgical Labiaplasty: What’s the Difference?

When comparing Aviva to a traditional surgical labiaplasty, the differences are significant. A traditional labiaplasty is an excisional procedure, meaning a surgeon uses a scalpel to cut away and remove excess tissue before stitching the area closed. While effective, this approach comes with the standard risks of surgery, including bleeding, infection, and visible scarring. Recovery can take several weeks, requiring significant downtime. Aviva, on the other hand, involves no tissue removal. Because it works beneath the skin to contract and tighten tissue, it avoids large incisions and leaves no linear scars. This leads to a much faster recovery—often just a few days—with less discomfort and a lower risk of complications.

### Understanding Traditional Surgical Labiaplasty

To fully appreciate what makes Aviva different, it helps to understand the traditional surgical approach. Surgical labiaplasty is an excisional procedure, meaning a surgeon uses a scalpel to physically cut and remove excess labial tissue. While it has been the standard for years and can produce excellent results, it is a more invasive process that requires a longer recovery. The specific surgical technique used often depends on your unique anatomy and your surgeon’s recommendation, as different methods achieve slightly different aesthetic outcomes. Understanding these methods gives you a clearer picture of the surgical path and how it compares to a non-surgical alternative.

#### Common Surgical Methods: Trim vs. Wedge

When it comes to surgical labiaplasty, surgeons typically use one of two primary techniques: the trim method or the wedge method. The trim technique involves making an incision along the outer edge of the labia minora to remove excess tissue, which can also address darker pigmentation along the border. The wedge technique, on the other hand, involves removing a V-shaped, full-thickness section from the most prominent part of the labia. The remaining edges are then sutured together, preserving the natural border for what many consider a more organic look. The best surgical method is always chosen based on your individual anatomy and aesthetic goals.

#### Anesthesia Options for Surgical Procedures

Because this is a surgical procedure, it requires anesthesia to keep you comfortable and pain-free. The choice usually comes down to either local or general anesthesia. With local anesthesia, the surgical area is completely numbed while you remain awake and aware. Many women and their doctors prefer this option due to its lower risk profile and quicker initial recovery. General anesthesia, which puts you completely to sleep for the surgery, is another option, often used for more extensive procedures or based on a patient’s personal preference. This is a key part of the pre-operative discussion, and your surgeon will help you decide on the safest and most appropriate type of anesthesia for your situation.

Many women find that their newfound comfort and confidence after the procedure actually enhances their overall sexual experience and well-being.

**Why should I choose Aviva instead of traditional surgery?** Women often choose Aviva because it offers beautiful, natural-looking results without the downsides of traditional surgery. The key differences are the recovery and the lack of scarring. With Aviva, there are no incisions, which means no linear scars. The recovery is also much faster, with most women returning to daily activities in just a couple of days instead of several weeks.
