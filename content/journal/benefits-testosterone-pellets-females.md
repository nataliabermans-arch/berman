## How Do Pellets Compare to Other Hormone Therapies?

When you start looking into hormone replacement, you’ll find there are several ways to get your body the support it needs. From daily creams to weekly patches, each method has its own routine. ### Pellets vs. Creams and Gels

Topical creams and gels are a common starting point for many women. The routine involves applying a measured dose to your skin every day. While this method can be effective, it often comes with a significant drawback: inconsistent hormone levels. Because the dose is absorbed daily, it can create a “rollercoaster” effect, where your hormone levels peak shortly after application and then drop off. This fluctuation can mean your symptoms—like fatigue or mood swings—aren’t managed as steadily. In contrast, pellets provide a continuous, stable release of hormones around the clock, helping you feel balanced day in and day out.

### Pellets vs. Patches and Pills

Patches and pills are other familiar options that require you to stay on a strict schedule, whether it’s taking a pill every day or changing a patch every few days. Forgetting a dose can disrupt your hormone levels and bring symptoms back. Pellet therapy, on the other hand, uses [bioidentical hormones](/biote-hormone-therapy/) that are molecularly identical to what your body naturally produces. These plant-based hormones are considered a more natural approach than some of the synthetic versions found in other delivery methods. This can lead to better results and fewer side effects for many women.

#### How Pellets Avoid Liver-Related Risks

When you take a hormone in pill form, it first travels through your digestive system and is processed by your liver before entering your bloodstream. This “first-pass effect” can put a strain on the liver and has been associated with an increased risk of certain health issues, including blood clots. Testosterone pellets offer a smarter, safer route. Because the pellet is inserted just under the skin, it releases a steady, stable dose of hormones directly into your bloodstream. This method completely bypasses the liver, avoiding that initial processing and its related risks. It’s a cleaner, more direct delivery system that allows your body to benefit from the therapy without placing an unnecessary burden on your liver, making it a preferred form of [bioidentical hormone replacement therapy](/biote-hormone-therapy/) for many women.
