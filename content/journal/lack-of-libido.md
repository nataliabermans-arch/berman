## Are Your Antidepressants Affecting Your Sex Drive?

Depression often co-exists with sexual dysfunction, and the medical treatment of depression can further worsen sexual symptoms or cause de-novo sexual dysfunction in a person who did not experience it prior to treatment. There are many drugs that can adversely affect sexual response. Among antidepressants, this effect is commonly observed with selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRI).

### How SSRIs Can Impact Sexual Response

If you’re taking an SSRI, you’re prioritizing your mental health—and that’s a powerful, important step. But it can feel frustrating when the medication that helps your mind seems to put a damper on your body. SSRIs work by increasing serotonin levels in the brain, which is great for managing depression and anxiety. However, this same chemical messenger plays a role in sexual function. For some women, this can lead to a decreased interest in sex, difficulty becoming aroused, or trouble reaching orgasm. It’s a well-known side effect, and you are definitely not alone in experiencing it. The key is understanding that you don’t have to simply accept this as your new normal or choose between your mental and sexual wellness.

### Addressing Urinary and Pelvic Health

Pelvic health is a cornerstone of a woman’s overall well-being, yet issues like urinary incontinence are often accepted as an inevitable part of aging or motherhood. Leaking when you laugh, sneeze, or exercise is not something you just have to live with. These concerns are typically linked to a weakened pelvic floor—the group of muscles that support your bladder, uterus, and bowel. When these muscles lose strength, it can affect bladder control and even diminish sexual sensation. Addressing pelvic floor health is crucial not only for managing urinary symptoms but also for improving your quality of life and restoring your confidence in everyday activities.

#### Strengthening the Pelvic Floor Non-Invasively

While Kegels are often recommended for strengthening the pelvic floor, they aren’t always enough to resolve significant issues like urinary incontinence. This non-invasive method provides a comprehensive workout for your pelvic muscles, effectively treating incontinence and improving pelvic tone far beyond what traditional exercises can achieve.

### Understanding Hypoactive Sexual Desire Disorder (HSDD)

If you find yourself constantly thinking, “I’m just never in the mood,” you are far from alone. This experience has a name: Hypoactive Sexual Desire Disorder, or HSDD. It’s recognized as the most common form of female sexual dissatisfaction and is defined by a persistent lack of desire or the absence of sexual thoughts and fantasies. This isn’t about a temporary dip in interest; it’s a consistent state where you rarely, if ever, feel the urge to initiate sex or respond to a partner’s advances.
