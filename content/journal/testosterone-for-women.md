Hormones are chemical messengers that are essential for the precise and flawless functioning of the body. They also strengthen bones, muscles, improve brain function and allow for a healthy and potent sex life. Nearly every biological process in the human body involves hormones, including the sexual function, growth, metabolism, weight control, blood pressures, and immune function of the individual. Estrogen plays an essential role in regulating memory, mood, sexual function, bone density and relief from common symptoms such as hot flashes and night sweats. Hormones and hormonal imbalance can have a significant impact a person’s mood, cognitive ability, short term memory and overall health of the brain.

Hormonal imbalance, due to drops in estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone occur when women enter perimenopause and/or menopause. During perimenopause and menopause, the production of estrogen, progesterone and testosterone production by the ovaries becomes irregular and eventually stops. Estrogen levels dropping can cause mood swings, anxiety, memory loss, and difficulty falling and/or staying asleep. It can cause weight gain, vaginal dryness and urinary frequency and urgency.

## What is Testosterone?

Testosterone is a fundamental hormone that is equally important for women as men. Testosterone plays an integral role in regulating your energy, lean muscle mass, and libido. It also helps to maintain bone density, fat distribution, muscle mass, muscle strength, sleep, and your physical energy levels. Women produce testosterone, just like men, but in smaller amounts. A drop in testosterone levels in women can lead these symptoms:

- Low sex drive
- Depression and/or anxiety
- Fatigue
- Inability to focus or concentrate.
- Decline in memory and cognitive skills
- Decreased muscle mass and strength
- Sagging Skin
- Insomnia
- Increased abdominal fat
- Psychological symptoms (mood swings, anxiety, lack of vitality and insomnia)

## What Causes Low Testosterone in Women?

Low testosterone levels can have a detrimental impact on overall health and bodily functions in both men and women. Testosterone levels continually change as women age and fluctuate during the menstrual cycle. However, as women age, testosterone levels significantly drop. Other common causes of low testosterone in women include:

- Long-term use of oral birth control pills and contraceptive patches.
- Pre-mature Ovarian failure
- Chemotherapy, radiation treatment, and eating disorders.
- Medications like anti-hypertensives and opiates.
- Tumors
- Hysterectomy
- Adrenal insufficiency
- Aging
- Peri-menopause and menopause

## What Are the Symptoms of Low Testosterone in Women?

Contrary to popular belief, low testosterone levels impact women as well. A drop in testosterone can cause health issues and emotional distress. Low testosterone levels in women is associated with the following symptoms:

- Hair loss
- Thinning and dry skin
- Trouble sleeping
- Overall decrease in muscle strength
- Reduced muscle tone.
- Weight gain, especially around the middle
- Problems with fertility
- Menstrual cycle irregularity
- Loss of bone density
- Fatigue for no obvious reason
- Less interest in sex and intimacy
- Mood changes, including feeling depressed or anxious
- Inability to concentrate
- Loss of “pep” or overall motivation

However, it is important to note that other conditions, associated with ageing and menopause, can also cause these symptoms and also need to be evaluated and addressed. These include hypothyroidism, iron deficiency, and depression.
