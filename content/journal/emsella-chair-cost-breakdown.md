### What Causes a Weak Pelvic Floor?

It’s easy to think a weak pelvic floor is caused by a single event, but it’s often the result of multiple factors over time. For many women, the most significant contributor is [pregnancy and childbirth](https://www.betterhealth.vic.gov.au/health/conditionsandtreatments/pelvic-floor), which places immense strain on these essential muscles. Things like chronic constipation, persistent heavy lifting, or even high-impact exercises can gradually weaken the pelvic floor. Other contributing factors can include obesity, which adds extra pressure to the pelvic region, and even traumatic injuries from an accident. Understanding these causes is the first step toward finding an effective solution for related issues like [incontinence](/emsella-treatment-for-incontinence/) or pelvic discomfort.

While Emsella is a safe and effective option for many, there are specific situations where the treatment is not recommended. It’s crucial to know these contraindications to protect your health. You remain fully clothed the entire time.

The results continue to improve for a few weeks following your final session as your muscles get stronger.

**Is this treatment only for women who have had children?** Absolutely not. While childbirth is a common cause of weakened pelvic floor muscles, it’s certainly not the only one. Incontinence and pelvic floor issues can affect women at various stages of life due to factors like aging, hormonal changes, or high-impact sports.
