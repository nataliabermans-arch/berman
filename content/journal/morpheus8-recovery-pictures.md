### Common Side Effects to Expect

Right after your session, you can expect your skin to look and feel a bit like you’ve had a mild sunburn. The most common side effects are some redness, minor swelling, and a feeling of warmth or tightness in the treated area. You might also notice some sensitivity or tenderness to the touch.

This initial healing phase is a crucial part of the skin remodeling process that leads to firmer, smoother skin.

### Your Post-Treatment Care Checklist

A great recovery starts with great aftercare. Your main goals are to keep your skin hydrated, protected, and free from irritation. For the first few days, treat your skin gently. Keep the area well-moisturized with a recommended product and avoid direct sun exposure. Applying a high-SPF, broad-spectrum sunscreen daily is non-negotiable to protect your healing skin.

It’s also important to avoid harsh skincare products for at least a week. Steer clear of ingredients like Retinol, Vitamin C, and glycolic acids. You should also hold off on using scrubs, exfoliants, or makeup for the first two to three days. Following these simple steps will help ensure your recovery is as smooth and quick as possible.

While most side effects are mild and short-lived, it’s important to listen to your body. Your provider is your partner in this process, and you should never hesitate to reach out if something doesn’t feel right. If you experience severe pain, excessive swelling that doesn’t improve, or any signs of infection like pus or spreading redness, contact your doctor immediately.
