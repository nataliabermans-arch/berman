Because there are no testosterone products specifically approved for women, doctors often prescribe male-approved testosterone in a practice known as “off-label” use. This simply means a drug is prescribed for a condition or at a dosage that isn’t specified on its official FDA label. This is a common and legal practice in medicine when a doctor believes a treatment is medically appropriate for their patient. For women, this involves prescribing much lower doses of [testosterone for women](/testosterone-for-women/) than would be used for men to address symptoms like low libido and fatigue, especially during menopause.
